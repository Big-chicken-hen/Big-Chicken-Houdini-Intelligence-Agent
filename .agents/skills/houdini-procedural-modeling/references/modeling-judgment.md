# Modeling Judgment

Use these heuristics to decide what the asset must communicate and whether its form is resolved.

## Set the completion level

- Treat a nontrivial named asset as a complete deliverable by default. Require target identity, coherent structure, meaningful editability, and evidence appropriate to its use.
- Treat an explicit blockout, proxy, placeholder, or technical test as limited-scope work. Establish only the requested scale, masses, proportions, or construction proof; do not silently promote it to a finished asset.
- Treat stylized or low-poly work as complete when simplification is intentional and the defining structural information remains. Polygon count alone does not make an asset a blockout.

## Capture target-specific information

Before selecting nodes, identify:

- the silhouette and proportions that establish identity;
- mandatory functional or structural components and their relationships;
- defining openings, negative spaces, attachments, transitions, and repeated patterns;
- the scale, viewing distance, and use context at which those features must read;
- which features are structural, stylistic, optional, or supplied by later LookDev.

Reject complexity that adds nodes without adding target information. If the construction could be reused almost unchanged for an unrelated subject, the asset is probably still generic.

## Resolve forms in order

1. Establish primary masses, silhouette, world scale, axes, ground or attachment plane, and major negative spaces.
2. Add secondary assemblies that explain construction, function, support, articulation, or recognizable organization.
3. Add tertiary seams, fasteners, trims, surface transitions, controlled variation, and other cues only after the larger relationships work.

Do not use small detail to distract from incorrect proportions or missing assemblies.

## Judge structural plausibility

- For manufactured or architectural work, resolve thickness, edge behavior, panel gaps, contacts, clearances, supports, openings, and plausible assembly separation.
- For organic or blended work, resolve large-form flow, anchors, branching or growth logic, and controllable shape before surface noise.
- For modular or environmental work, preserve connection rules, scale consistency, repetition logic, boundary conditions, and usable variants.
- Keep material regions or semantic groups available when they matter to later LookDev, without turning modeling into material authoring.

## Attribute visible problems

- Assign silhouette or proportion errors to primary construction.
- Assign floating, penetrating, unsupported, or implausibly joined parts to assembly and contact logic.
- Assign repetitive artifacts to source, placement, orientation, spacing, collision, or variation rules rather than randomizing more properties.
- Distinguish model problems from camera perspective, lighting, material response, or post-processing before rebuilding geometry.
- If the subject reads only because of labels or materials, verify that its modeled structure carries enough identity on its own.
